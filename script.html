02
Customer Verification
Confirm identity before discussing account details
▾
"Before I go any further, I just need to run through a couple of quick security questions to confirm I'm speaking with the right person — is that okay?

Could you confirm your full name and the first line of your address, including your postcode?"
ℹ️Do not read out account details until the customer has passed verification. If they fail, politely advise them to call back via the number on their bill.
Verification Objections
❓"Why do you need my details — how do I know you're genuine?"▾
"That's a really sensible question — I'm glad you asked. I'm not asking for any payment details or passwords. I just need to confirm your name and address to make sure I'm speaking with the account holder before I can discuss anything on your account. If you'd prefer, you're very welcome to call us back on the number on your energy bill and we can arrange this there."
03
Eligibility & Meter Check
Confirm which meters need upgrading
▾
"I can see on your account that you currently have a traditional meter fitted. We're reaching out to all eligible customers to arrange an upgrade to a smart meter at no cost to you.

Just to confirm — can you tell me where your electricity and/or gas meter is located, for example in a cupboard, on an outside wall, or in the garage? This helps our engineer plan the visit."
⚠️If the property is rented, the customer must confirm they have landlord permission before booking. Note this on the account.
Common Questions
❓"I already have a smart meter — why are you calling?"▾
"I apologise for the confusion — it's possible our records haven't been updated yet. Let me make a note of that for you and we'll update the account. There's nothing further you need to do."
❓"I rent — do I need to ask my landlord?"▾
"Yes, for rented properties we do ask that you get written or verbal permission from your landlord first — this is just to protect you. Many landlords are happy to agree as smart meters can actually help with property management. Once you've spoken to them, please give us a call back and we can get this booked in for you."
04
Benefits & Objection Handling
Value proposition — keep it conversational, not pushy
▾
"A smart meter upgrade is completely free and has some great benefits for you.

You'll get accurate bills — no more estimates — because the meter sends readings directly to your supplier. You'll also get a free in-home display that shows you in real time how much energy you're using and what it's costing, which many customers find helps them cut down on their bills.

The whole installation takes around 1.5 to 2.5 hours and our engineer will need brief access to your meter. There'll be a short power interruption of around 5 minutes during the electric meter switch, so we'd just ask you to save any work on computers beforehand."
Objection Handling
❓"I don't want a smart meter — I've heard bad things."▾
"I completely understand — there's been a lot of mixed information out there. The early generation of smart meters (SMETS1) did have some issues when switching supplier, but the meters we're installing now are second-generation SMETS2, which work across all suppliers. You're also still in control — you can opt out of automatic data sharing and just send monthly reads if you prefer. It's worth giving it a try, and if you change your mind after installation we can look at your options."
❓"Will there be any cost to me?"▾
"No — the installation is completely free of charge. There's no cost for the meter, the engineer visit, or the in-home display. It's provided as part of the government's smart meter rollout programme."
❓"I'm worried about health / radiation / hacking."▾
"That's a concern we do hear, and it's worth addressing. Smart meters use very low-level radio signals — similar to a standard Wi-Fi router or baby monitor — and they meet all UK government and EU safety standards. In terms of data security, the smart meter network uses end-to-end encryption and is regulated by Ofgem. Your energy data is only shared with your supplier."
❓"I'm not in — can someone else be there?"▾
"Yes — as long as the person present is over 18 and is the account holder or has the account holder's permission, that's absolutely fine. We'd just need you to let us know on the booking notes."
❓"I don't have time / too busy."▾
"I completely understand — life gets busy. We have appointments available across a wide range of dates and we offer morning and afternoon slots, including some Saturdays. Is there a particular time of year or day of the week that works better for you? I can hold a provisional date and call you back to confirm once you've had a chance to check your diary."
